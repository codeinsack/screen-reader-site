Screen Reader Extension

About This Extension

Screen Reader Extension is a Google Chrome browser extension designed to make digital content more accessible by reading text aloud using advanced text-to-speech (TTS) technology. This extension helps users who prefer audio content, have reading difficulties, or need hands-free text consumption.

Purpose and Functionality

The primary purpose of this extension is to provide an accessible reading experience by converting on-screen text into spoken words. The extension reads text content from web pages and Google Documents, making digital documents accessible through audio playback.

Key Features:
  • Reads text aloud from Google Documents using natural-sounding voices
  • Reads content from web pages you visit
  • Provides control over playback (play, pause, stop)
  • Supports multiple languages
  • Adjustable reading speed and voice selection
  • Enhances accessibility for users with visual impairments or reading difficulties

Why We Need Google Documents Access

This extension requests the https://www.googleapis.com/auth/documents.readonly permission to:

  • Read the text content from your Google Documents
  • Convert the document text into speech for audio playback
  • Provide an accessible reading experience without requiring you to copy-paste text manually

Important: The extension uses a read-only scope, which means it can only read your document content. It cannot modify, delete, or share your documents. Your documents remain completely private and secure.

How It Works

When you open a Google Document, the extension detects the document content and offers to read it aloud. You can control the reading experience through the extension's interface, including play, pause, and stop controls. The extension uses your browser's built-in text-to-speech capabilities to convert text into natural-sounding speech.

Privacy and Security

We take your privacy seriously. The extension only accesses the text content necessary for reading aloud. No data is stored, transmitted to external servers, or shared with third parties. All processing happens locally in your browser.
